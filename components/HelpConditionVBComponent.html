<div class="help-content">
  <h1>Conditions (VB.NET Style)</h1>
  A condition is just a Boolean expression -- an expression that is either <b>true</b> or <b>false</b>.
  <ul>
    <li>Conditions are used to define selection and repetition control structures</li>
    <li>In Jeroo, a Boolean expression is any valid combination of Boolean operators
      and invocations of Boolean methods.</li>
    <li>There are two Boolean constants: <b>TRUE</b> and <b>FALSE</b></li>
    <li>There are three Boolean operators:
      <font size="3" color="#000000" face="courier"><b>NOT</b>,
        <b> AND</b>, and <b>OR</b>.</font><br></li>
    <li>In the absence of parentheses to indicate otherwise,
      every NOT operator will be evaluated first,
      every AND operator will be evaluated second, and
      every OR operator will be evaluated third.</li>
  </ul>
  <br>
  <br>

            </td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td width="5%"> </td>
            <td>Suppose that <b>P</b> represents a Boolean expression.<br>
              The expression <b>NOT P</b> has the opposite truth value.
              <br>
              <br>
              <pre>Code example:
                <b>NOT Kim.isWater(LEFT) </b></pre>
            </td>

            <td>Suppose that <b>P</b> and <b>Q</b>
              represent Boolean expressions.<br>
              The expression<b> P AND Q</b> is only true when
              both <b>P</b> and <b>Q</b> are true.
              <br>
              <br>
              <pre>Code example:
                <b>Kim.hasFlower() AND Kim.isNet(AHEAD)</b></pre>
            </td>
